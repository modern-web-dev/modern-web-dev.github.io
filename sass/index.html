Sass

  • It is a preprocessor scripting language that is compiled into Cascading Style Sheets*
  • And it comes with two syntax flavours**

Sass setup

  • Step #1 - install Node.js LTS edition *
  • Step #2 - install Sass CLI
    • > npm install -g sass
  • Step #3 - let's begin to compile Sass files
    • > sass src/index.scss dist/index.css

In real world projects we mostly won't take these steps. We'll use a bundler or a JS framework instead.

Numbers

  • May or may not have units, like 12 or 100px
  • You can mix compatible units like in with px
    @debug 4px * 6px; // 24px*px (read "square pixels")
  • While dividing the same units used in numerator and denominator, they will cancel out

Strings

  • may or may not have quotes, like "Helvetica Neue" or bold.

Colors

  • can be referred to by their hex representation or by name, like #c6538c or blue, or returned from functions, like rgb(107, 113, 127) or hsl(210, 100%, 20%).

Lists

  • may be separated by spaces or commas and which may be enclosed in square brackets or no brackets at all, like 1.5em 1em 0 2em, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif, or [col1-start].

Module pattern

  • It's reasonable to extract common codebase into separate partial files. E.g. _variables.scss, _mixins.scss, _classes.scss
  • Import them with @use at-rule,
    e. g. @use './common/variables' as var
  • @import at-rule is also allowed, although it is obsolete and highly discouraged.
